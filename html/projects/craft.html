CRAFT

Benson, Matt. “Aerial View of Field.” Photo by Matt Benson on Unsplash, 30 Jan. 2015, https://unsplash.com/photos/MKLRMeKctI4.

An economical solution

  Developed by the Lightsmith group, CRAFT is a growth equity fund to be the first private sector investment fund for developing technologies to combat climate change and adaptation in developing and developed countries. The fund will go towards companies, who have been proven in the climate sphere, to develop new technologies and get involved in different geographical markets. This will help developing countries get technologies such as weather analytics and catastrophe risk modeling services.
  CRAFT suggests that for every dollar of congressional financing will lead to about 3.3 dollars of commercial investment for businesses. With their focus on business, CRAFT sheds light on an aspect of the climate change movement not normally talked about. Some people might be more responsive to this investment approach for impacting our carbon footprint. CRAFT grows the global economy by using the invested money to help carbon reducing products reach a global market. CRAFT has regional partnerships around the world to be able to create this market. Two thirds of the investments will go to developing countries since there is room for more growth in these countries. The technology reaches these countries through a mechanical system. The technology includes water harvesting, irrigation systems, and geospatial mapping.
