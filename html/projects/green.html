Green Climate Fund

“Green Climate Fund Bonn.” L-A-V-A, 2012, http://www.l-a-v-a.net/projects/green-climate-fund-bonn/. Accessed 2 Mar. 2022.

Global projects by private businesses

  Established by over 190 countries, the Green Climate Fund was established as a global program that managed investments from these countries to start initiatives that reduce our climate impact. The Green Climate Fund’s goal is to reduce greenhouse gas emissions in developing countries and protect the environment from current climate change damage. The Green Climate Fund currently has 190 projects across the globe with some country specific and some involving multiple countries. Most of these projects are global with there being 146 planned global projects.
  Financed mainly through grants and loans, the fund has currently implemented 155 of its scheduled projects. With 10 billion USD worth of investments, the fund will return 37.1 billion dollars with their developments. So far, about 2 billion tons of carbon have been offset through the efforts of the fund. Most of the money is put towards energy generation projects while most of the projects are for livelihoods.
  Some of their projects include improvements on cooling systems through the Cooling Facility. The Cooling Facility will be the first facility focused on providing nine developing countries with cooling systems. With focus on regulation, the Cooling Facility will figure out how to distribute functional cooling systems to these countries. The GCF will provide financing to develop climate-friendly cooling systems.
  Another of their projects is a rehabilitation project of Mexico’s environment. The GCF has recognized that climate change has caused damage like landslides to be more common. Climate change has increased so much now that certain areas are being affected by this landsliding. Therefore, this project is aimed at restoring the land and rivers affected by climate change to prevent this. This project is currently under implementation.
