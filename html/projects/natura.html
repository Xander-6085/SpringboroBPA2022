Natura Cosmetics

Reuters, Thomson. “Natura Cosmetics.” Fashion Network, 25 Mar. 2020, https://ww.fashionnetwork.com/news/Natura-to-convert-makeup-fragrances-production-to-hygiene-items-in-coronavirus-fight,1201020.html. Accessed 2 Mar. 2022.

An innovative business for a reocurring problem

  Natura Cosmetics is one of the largest cosmetics retailers in Brazil, and in 2007, they launched their Carbon Neutral Programme. With its creation, Natura Cosmetics has pledged to measure and control their carbon footprint. It has reduced gas emissions by almost 33%, and in turn, eliminated air travel when not necessary. All plastic that is used by the company is recycled plastic. Any carbon emissions that the company itself cannot offset is then offset by one of the company’s 32 carbon footprint projects across Brazil.
  Natura Cosmetics has reduced its carbon emissions using a carbon emission calculator for all of their products. The way it works is that every item in their inventory has an estimated footprint that they use to calculate how much emissions they need to offset through their climate projects. This has reduced the climate impact of making their products thus leading the company to be at net zero emissions.
  Natura Cosmetics climate projects are taking place in the Cerrado biome of Brazil. This area is predicted to be hurt by climate change leading to reduced water sources and possible extinction of about 38-45% of the plant life in the region. This is where their 33 carbon footprint projects come in. One of Natura Cosmetics’ climate projects has regrown over 500 hectares of rainforest in the Amazon - the Cerrado biome being part of the Amazon. Another project has formed a regenerative economy through coffee beans for their products.
  Natura Cosmetics even offers a way for its customers to be a part of reducing the company’s carbon footprint. Natura Cosmetics is the first to offer refills for all of their products. This is extremely beneficial as that means Natura Cosmetics does not have to remake the containers and bottles that hold their products thus reducing their climate impact.
  Natura Cosmetics efforts have not been for nothing. The company is net-zero and has offset 3.4 million tons of carbon emissions. Their 32 climate projects have not only helped the environment but also create over a 1000 jobs. Due to their success in the climate sphere, many other companies have been interested in copying Natura’s methods to reduce their own climate emissions.
