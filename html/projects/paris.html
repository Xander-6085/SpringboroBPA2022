An international agreement

Rivers cleaned through global cooperation

  The Paris Agreement is a worldwide treaty between 196 countries to reduce greenhouse gas emissions to keep global warming from reaching 2 degrees celsius. By mid-century, countries are supposed to peak global emissions to prevent any more global warming. The goal is that countries will return to pre-industrial levels of emissions.
  Every five years, countries will submit what they have done to reach their goal through nationally determined contributions or NDCs. Countries will have to invest money into not only technology to reduce climate change but also into resilience against climate change’s impact. The Paris Agreement builds a financial framework to help all the countries a part of the treaty help each other.
  In accordance with working together, by 2020, countries are invited to submit long-term low greenhouse gas emission development strategies to construct a way to deal with climate change long term. They are not mandatory, but the hope is that countries will develop new solutions while fulfilling their NDCs.
  Since climate change affects us all globally, all countries including developing countries need to be better equipped to deal with climate change. The agreement suggests that developed countries should voluntarily invest in other countries to help the global issue.
  The Paris Agreement is tracking progress through countries establishing an enhanced transparency framework. By 2024, countries will use these ETFs to be open about how they are progressing. All the information gathered will be put into a global collective to assess how each country is doing.
