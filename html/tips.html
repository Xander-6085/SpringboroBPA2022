Transportation Advice

  1. Drive Less

    2. Automobiles produce roughly 1/3rd of America's carbon emissions. By using alternative modes of transport, such as walking, biking, or public transportation, you greatly reduce your carbon footprint. Whenever you want to travel somewhere that would be too far without a car, you should consider carpooling or even reconsider going at all. The more you support local businesses the better!

  2. Accelerate and Brake only when Necessary

    3. Cars produce a lot more emissions when they are forced to use their engine more, and frequently hitting the gas or brake pedals runs the engine much faster than standard driving. Consider accelerating only gradually, and letting your car roll to a stop. Not only will this reduce your gas usage, it will also make your rides a whole lot smoother.

    Prototype for low emissions aircraft

  3. Fly Less

    4. Planes are notorious for producing plenty of carbon emissions that are dangerous to the environment. Even with their large passenger capacity they still pollute much more than cars. By flying only when necessary, you help avoid high travel costs, and the Earth's destruction.

  4. Plan Shorter Routes

    5. Before leaving, always check your route to make sure it is the quickest available. Just a few minutes shorter can add up over long periods of time, and you can arrive at your destination faster.

Home Advice

  1. Take Shorter, Colder Showers

    2. Of all of our daily appliances, showers use up the most water. To reduce this damage, take shorter showers. The less time you are in the shower, the less water will be used, and the less water that needs to be heated. A large portion of the electricity that we use comes from heating up water for our sinks and showers. Taking cold showers practically eliminates this extra energy usage, and can save you money on your monthly bills.

  2. Eat Local

    3. Privacy curtains

    Many of our carbon emissions are not directly produced by us, but are from the companies that produce the food we buy. By eating food produced locally, you can greatly reduce the emissions produced by your food's creation. While most mega-corporations produce their products with massive, inhumane, and disgusting animal factories, local farms typically use old-fashioned methods that don't use as much fertilizer or pesticides. Also eating local helps inspire business in your community and helps your neighbors.

  3. Turn Off Appliances not in Use

    4. By being economical with what electricity you use, you can both reduce your electricity bill and decrease the amount of fossil fuels used to generate our power. When not in a room, turn the lights off. If you are very serious about decreasing your environmental impact, you could even live from sun up to sun down to not use lights whatsoever. Other appliances tend to use a lot of electricity as well, such as the dishwasher, oven, microwave, computer, and telephone. Make sure you don't accidentally leave one of these on, as they can suck up your power very quickly.

  4. Let Sunlight In

    5. By opening windows, curtains, and drapes, you can use a lot less energy with your home's lightbulbs. In addition, consider replacing your incandescent light bulbs with compact fuorescent light bulbs (CFLs). These bulbs are much more economical, use less energy, and last longer. If you live in urban areas and need privacy, consider buying special curtains that allow sunlight in but still block visible light.

Technology Advice

  1. Store Data Locally

    2. As you can imagine, massive datacenters make the mass majority of technology-related carbon emissions. The main way common people use these datacenters is through cloud storage. Common programs such as Google Drive, Dropbox, Youtube, and GitHub store your data in their servers, which not only uses a lot more power but also reduces your right to your own data.

  2. Remove Unused Chargers

    3. Whenever your devices are finished charging, remove the charger from the outlet. Although it isn't actively draining energy to charge things, residual energy is still lost due to inefficent cable design. A suprising amount of energy is lost to heat by unused chargers.

  3. Turn off your Computer and Router

    4. During periods of inactivity, shut down your computer and router. Especially at night, computers and routers are not commonly used and yet they still consume just as much power as if they are on during the day. You can even set times for your personal computer to turn itself off if you forget.

Consumption Advice

    Reusable bags

  1. Purchase Second Hand Clothing

    2. Clothing is typically produced in massive Chinese or African factories that both use child labor and plenty of energy. Buying clothes made locally or clothes already used reduces the amount of money you give to these operations and somewhat helps shut them down. Also, many of the materials used to make clothing are not recyclable or at least hard to reuse, so buying already worn clothing can reduce landfill waste.

  2. Use Reusable Bags

    3. Paper and plastic bags have been notorious for their negative impact on our carbon emissions over the past 50 years. Not to mention plastic bags make up a lot of the trash in landfills, the ocean, and our streets. Using reusable bags is a good alternative that is fairly cheap, more reliable, and stylish.
